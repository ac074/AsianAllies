Reporting Hate Crime

  1. Ensure Safety: If you or anyone else is in immediate danger due to a hate crime, your top priority should be to ensure safety. Call 911 or your local emergency number right away to get immediate assistance. It is crucial to protect yourself and others around you from harm and to seek help from emergency responders.
  2. Gather Evidence: Carefully document every detail of the incident as soon as you can. This includes noting the exact date, time, and location where the event took place. If it is safe to do so, take clear photos or videos of any damage or injuries. Additionally, save any relevant messages, emails, or social media posts.
  1. Report to Local Authorities: Report the incident to your local police department as soon as possible. Provide them with a detailed account of what happened, including all the evidence you have collected. Make sure to obtain a copy of the police report for your records, which will be essential for any further legal actions
  2. Reach Out to Specialized Agencies: Beyond filing a police report, you should also report the incident to specialized agencies that deal with hate crimes, such as the FBI or civil rights organizations. These agencies offer additional resources and support, and play a crucial role in tracking hate crimes on a national level.
